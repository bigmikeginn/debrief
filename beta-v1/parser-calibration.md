# Parser Calibration

## Current Rule

Debrief parsing is faithful-first:

1. Preserve the concrete technique details from the raw note.
2. Keep the main visible summary specific and searchable.
3. Add coach interpretation only when it is directly supported by the note.
4. Mark vague notes with lower confidence instead of filling gaps.
5. Do not collapse different notes into generic slogans.

## Banned Pattern

Do not turn specific mechanics into broad phrases like:

- Build connection first.
- Load weight before moving.
- Move when resistance lightens.
- Choose one main lesson.
- Pick the next training focus.

## Calibration Examples

### Back Attack Control

Raw note:

> When attacking from the back Ensure that you are preventing their access to the ground by tucking your knee or elbow underneath them to make escape more difficult. Also your uppermost control leg can post on their hip rather than their inner thigh to give you additional leverage and control and if they escape their top leg you can always hook their bottom leg for a secondary control

Better saved summary:

> Block their access to the mat by tucking a knee or elbow underneath them. Post the upper control leg on the hip for leverage, then switch to bottom-leg control if they free the top leg.

### Bow And Arrow Defense

Raw note:

> When defending against the bow and arrow choke take your arm on the same side that your pants are being grabbed and inserted inside and around the pant grabbing arm with an under hook. You can then pull their hand off your pant or if the hand remains it is possible to arm bar the offending arm. With long enough limbs you might also be able to reach up and pull on your own collar further reducing the pressure

Better saved summary:

> Use the same-side arm to underhook around the pant-gripping arm, peel the grip, or threaten the armbar. If reachable, pull your own collar to reduce choke pressure.

### Rear Naked Choke Defense

Raw note:

> When defending against the rear naked choke look into the choking elbow and bicep instead of away from it. This will free up one of your carotid arteries from pressure and give you time to start to escape. It is also very important to make sure your head gets to the opposite side of the other person's head so they can't push you into the choking arm

Better saved summary:

> Turn toward the choking elbow and bicep to relieve carotid pressure, then move your head to the opposite side of theirs so they cannot drive you back into the choking arm.
